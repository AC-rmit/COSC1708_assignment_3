Reflection

Group Reflection

Compared to our previous assignment, we started off with a higher production rate at the beginning now that we were familiar with each other. With feedback from Anthony regarding our teamwork and assignment 2, implemented a two-person per task system in order to expose ourselves to more opportunities to work together outside of meetings and ensure progress does not halt with individual members were unable to attend or contribute for a time. This worked particularly well when Sahil had a leave of absence due to family reasons, and so Mihir was capable of continuing where Sahil left off and other members could contribute to make up the loss of a member.

An ongoing issue from the last assignment may be the hesitation in communicating, even with the new assignment task system. This may be due to other commitments each member of the team had, whether it was personal or academic clashing. This resulted in some progress of researching and developing artefacts to be somewhat delayed, though not as grievously as last the assignment, as the report and HTML section has been completed with more time to spare this time around.

Productivity does seem to increase with regular meeting updates, as these meetings over Microsoft Teams gave us the opportunity to discuss other avenues of research and development. Working in one file that can be updated live is partially useful as members can correct and add to report content in real-time and ask for active feedback in case writing processes come to a halt. Though the most pleasant surprise were some “Eureka” moments when discussing avenues of technology research, as on more than one occasion we made leaps and bounds in progress from one member simply discussing his current knowledge and another member commenting on a resource that they know of that could be instrumental in furthering the assignment goals. Pooling together collective knowledge may be a more productive means to move forward as future assignment progresses, even if it is just a run-off discussion of previous experiences.

Regards to skills and experiences already brought to the table and developed during this assignment. Adam’s Figma experience brought the visual component of our app to life, Aby’s dedication to improving database integration with API made leaps and bounds, Sahil and Mihir brought the structure of our report to fruition, with Mihir carefully bringing Sahil’s portion up to speed. Xiaolong’s coding experience gave more weight to our plans and progress for his research into Google Maps and how it can be used for our app. Lastly, Aaron’s previous experience in database management got the usage of SQL and databases for the app off the ground.

Individual Reflection

Aaron

This time around I believe our ability to cooperate has improved since our second assignment, as even after a few of our members were unable to attend sessions for reasons outside of their control we managed to keep on track on developing our IT artefacts and reports at a stable pace. Initiating multiple members in multiple tasks pushed our members to actively seek each other out for help and status updates and, when a member was out of reach, the tasks were at least handled by someone else who was assigned so progress wouldn’t come to a halt.

I found it surprising the amount of research could be revealed from a collaborative discussion as opposed to an individual research assignment, as compared to last assignment where little crossover took place.

I would say the main difficulty of this assignment would be one that also occurred in the previous, lack of confidence in speaking up ideas and constructive criticism. Perhaps to be expected since at most meetings I took up a lead speaking role due to hesitancy from other members. The others have improved in communicating in and out of classes and meetings, however, trying to attend and speak at the consultations and coming up with very good initiatives for the project. Improving confidence in our own lines of thinking can lead to more active teamwork and hopefully we will gain such confidence as our studies go on.

Adam

The main issues were still prevalent from assignment 2 in a lesser form being time management and communication. I believe with more planning and more focused targeting the issue of time management that will be able to be improve, and communication may be improved through everyone talking about their different parts more in each group meeting or at least semi frequent updates. I’m sure everyone in this group working in the future will be able to gain confidence which can lead to even better teamwork and be able to lay out great plans for working in groups.
